Miso Soup

Miso soup is a staple in Japanese cuisine and is soup for the soul. We enjoy it almost every single day for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. As a Japanese home cook, I would also say that miso soup is probably one of the easiest soups you can make at home.

Ingredients

  • Kombu (dried kelp)
  • Katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes)
  • Tofu (soft or silken)
  • Miso paste
  • Dried Wakame Seaweed
  • Scallion or Green Onion

Steps

  • Dashi

    1. Gently clean kombu
    2. Make slits in kombu to release flavour
    3. Cold brew kombu if you have time (2-3 hours), otherwise add dried kombu to medium saucepan
    4. Turn on heat to medium-low and bring to near boil, about 10 minutes
    5. Clean from/debris from surface all along
    6. Just before boiling, remove kombu from pan (it can be saved for further recipes)
    7. Add kastuobushi to water and return to near boiling
    8. Once boiling, reduce heat and let simmer for 30 seconds, then remove from heat
    9. Allow katsuobushi to sink to bottom of water, about 10 minutes
    10. Strain katsuobushi (it can also be saved)
    11. Dashi is ready to use or be stored (it will keep for 3-5 days refrigerated)

  • Miso Soup

    1. Add miso paste to dashi (approx 1 tbsp to 200ml dashi)
    2. Stir miso paste until dissolved in dashi
    3. Cube tofu and add tofu to soup
    4. Add wakame and allow to loosen until soft
    5. Remove from heat and serve, adding green onion to bowl to taste
